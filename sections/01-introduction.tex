\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

\IEEEPARstart{N}{avigating} dynamic, human-populated environments is a fundamental challenge in embodied AI and mobile robotics. A truly autonomous home or service robot must not only avoid physical collisions but also adhere to implicit social norms. This includes maintaining a polite distance from a conversation, yielding to a person approaching from a blind corner, or proactively responding to semantic auditory events such as a doorbell ringing or a human calling for help. While humans naturally rely on a rich, synchronized fusion of visual and acoustic cues to navigate these complex social dynamics, current robotic navigation systems often process these modalities in isolation, severely limiting their social intelligence.

Existing approaches to human-aware navigation broadly fall into two decoupled paradigms. On one hand, \textit{Social Navigation} predominantly relies on line-of-sight visual sensors (RGB-D, LiDAR) to detect pedestrians and forecast their trajectories. However, these systems are fundamentally ``deaf''; they cannot anticipate a person walking behind a wall via footstep sounds or recognize a stationary group engaged in conversation, often leading to intrusive, startling, or unsafe behaviors. On the other hand, \textit{Audio-Visual Navigation} (AVNav) leverages binaural or multi-channel audio to locate sound sources (e.g., AudioGoal). Yet, these methods typically treat audio purely as a geometric target to reach or as background noise to ignore. They lack the \textit{social awareness} required to treat specific human acoustic activities as dynamic virtual boundaries or social constraints.

To bridge the gap between deaf social navigation and socially-unaware audio-visual navigation, we introduce SAVNav (Socially-Aware Audio-Visual Navigation). SAVNav is a comprehensive sensorimotor framework that elevates ambient sounds from mere physical signals to semantic social constraints. Recognizing that our system requires innovations across perception, mapping, and planning, we architect SAVNav as a tightly integrated pipeline.

In the \textit{perception} module, we integrate first-order Ambisonics acoustic positioning (via Embed-ACCDOA~\cite{OpenvocabularySoundEvent2025}) with RGB-D visual abstraction (YOLO and ImageBind) to extract both the spatial location and semantic category of sound events and human activities. These multi-modal streams are then dynamically fused into \textit{SAVMap}, a novel spatio-temporal representation that features a robust sparse belief anchoring mechanism for multi-modal alignment and a topology-aware hallucinated momentum algorithm. This allows the system to anticipate unseen human movements (NLOS) from ambient sounds, mapping them into dynamic, anisotropic social cost fields. Finally, our \textit{planning} layer employs an uncertainty-aware social navigation policy. Rather than passively following geometric shortest paths, the robot proactively explores the environment to resolve acoustic ambiguities of the target, while simultaneously anticipating and yielding to the dynamic social regions defined by human activities.

The main contributions of this work are summarized as follows:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{A Novel Benchmark for Socially-Aware Audio-Visual Navigation:} We introduce a new task where embodied agents must locate semantic acoustic targets while simultaneously interpreting ambient human sounds (e.g., conversations, footsteps) as dynamic social constraints. To support this, we develop a high-fidelity continuous simulation environment with multi-agent human dynamics and complex acoustic rendering, accompanied by a comprehensive dataset and new social compliance metrics.
    \item \textbf{Acoustic-to-Spatial Social Mapping (SAVMap):} We propose \textit{SAVMap}, a spatio-temporal representation that transforms human-generated sounds, including non-line-of-sight acoustic events, into persistent spatial social constraints. By fusing audio observations with visual cues and scene topology, SAVMap enables the robot to infer hidden social boundaries and approaching human risks before visual confirmation.
    \item \textbf{Proactive Socially-Aware Navigation (SAVNav):} We design \textit{SAVNav}, a navigation policy that uses SAVMap to proactively yield to inferred social boundaries while actively disambiguating uncertain acoustic observations when necessary, enabling robust target reaching in dynamic human environments.
    \item \textbf{Extensive Evaluation:} Extensive evaluations in our simulated benchmark and deployments on a physical Stretch 3 robot demonstrate that SAVNav significantly outperforms state-of-the-art visual-only and audio-visual baselines in navigation success, search efficiency, and social compliance metrics.
\end{itemize}
