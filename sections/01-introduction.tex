\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

\IEEEPARstart{N}{avigating} dynamic, human-populated environments remains a basic challenge in embodied AI and mobile robotics. A home or service robot must avoid physical collisions and respect implicit social norms. That includes keeping distance from a conversation, yielding to a person approaching from a blind corner, and responding to an acoustic target such as a doorbell or a call for help. Humans routinely combine visual and acoustic cues in these settings, but current navigation systems usually process the modalities separately, which limits their ability to reason about social context.

Existing approaches to human-aware navigation largely fall into two separate lines. Social navigation (SocialNav) relies on line-of-sight visual sensors such as RGB-D or LiDAR to detect pedestrians and forecast trajectories. These systems cannot anticipate a person hidden behind a wall from footstep sounds, nor can they recognize a stationary group engaged in conversation before it is visible. Audio-visual navigation (AVNav), by contrast, uses binaural or multi-channel audio to locate sound sources, but usually treats audio as a target to reach or as background noise to ignore. It does not model social acoustic events as dynamic social boundaries.

To address this gap, we introduce SAVNav, a framework for socially-aware audio-visual navigation. As shown in Fig.~\ref{fig:teaser}, SAVNav treats social acoustic events as spatial constraints rather than isolated signals. The system is organized around perception, mapping, and planning.

In the perception module, we combine first-order Ambisonics (FOA) acoustic positioning via embed-ACCDOA~\cite{OpenvocabularySoundEvent2025} with RGB-D visual abstraction from YOLO26 and ImageBind to recover both the spatial location and semantic category of sound events and human activities. These streams are fused into SAVMap, a spatio-temporal representation with sparse audio-visual anchoring and topology-aware acoustic anticipation. SAVMap lets the system anticipate non-line-of-sight (NLOS) dynamic pedestrians from social acoustic events and map them into dynamic, anisotropic social cost fields. The planning layer then uses the SAVNav policy to explore when the acoustic target remains uncertain and to yield when social boundaries appear.

The main contributions are summarized as follows:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textit{A Benchmark for the SAVNav Task:} We define a task in which an embodied agent must locate an acoustic target while treating social acoustic events, such as conversations and footsteps, as dynamic social boundaries. We support the task with a continuous simulation environment, multi-agent human dynamics, acoustic rendering, and social compliance metrics.
    \item \textit{Acoustic-to-Spatial Social Mapping (SAVMap):} We propose SAVMap, a spatio-temporal representation that turns human-generated sounds, including non-line-of-sight (NLOS) events, into persistent spatial social constraints. By combining audio observations, visual cues, and scene topology, SAVMap enables the robot to infer NLOS social boundaries and dynamic pedestrians before visual confirmation.
    \item \textit{Proactive Socially-Aware Navigation Policy (SAVNav policy):} We design the SAVNav policy, a planning module that uses SAVMap to yield to inferred social boundaries while actively disambiguating uncertain acoustic targets when needed.
    \item \textit{Evaluation:} We evaluate SAVNav in simulation and on a physical Stretch 3 robot, and show improvements in navigation success, search efficiency, and social compliance over visual-only and audio-visual baselines.
\end{enumerate}
