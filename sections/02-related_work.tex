\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\subsection{Vision-Based Social Navigation}
Traditional and learning-based social navigation (SocialNav) systems optimize paths to prevent physical collisions and respect implicit social norms \cite{PrinciplesGuidelinesEvaluating2025, Habitat30Cohabitat2023}. While tracking-based approaches forecast pedestrian trajectories to proactively yield space \cite{SocialNavTrainingHumanInspired2026, CognitionPrecognitionFutureaware2025}, recent methodologies employ vision-language models (VLMs) to evaluate path etiquette \cite{VLMsocialnavSociallyAware2025, ObstaclesEtiquetteRobot2026}. However, these vision-centric policies inherently depend on line-of-sight (LOS) sensors. Unable to perceive non-line-of-sight (NLOS) risks, they fail to anticipate a dynamic pedestrian approaching from a blind corner or yield to unobserved human activities, often causing abrupt boundary violations when entities suddenly fall into LOS.

\subsection{Audio-Visual Navigation}
AVNav equips agents with omnidirectional auditory perception to overcome restricted visual fields. Initial research proved sound effectively guides agents to static acoustic targets in unexplored spaces \cite{LookListenAct2020, SoundSpaces20Simulation2022, ObjectNavRevisitedEvaluation2020}. Beyond continuous signals, recent frameworks localize transient semantic events \cite{SemanticAudiovisualNavigation2021}, track moving sources \cite{CatchMeIf2023}, and handle noisy, multi-source environments via spatial reasoning or multi-agent collaboration \cite{AudiovisualNavigationNoisy2025, AdvancingAudiovisualNavigation2025, ExperimentalStudyJoint2025, MVANetMultistageVideo2025, OmnidirectionalInformationGathering2023}. Advanced architectures also integrate foundation models for zero-shot semantic reasoning \cite{RILAReflectiveImaginative2024, CAVENEmbodiedConversational2024, BATLearningReason2024, AudioguidedVisualPerception2025} or pinpoint domestic emergencies using audio cues \cite{HomeEmergencyUsingAudio2025}. However, these approaches treat non-target sounds exclusively as alternative goals or background noise to be attenuated. Consequently, they fail to model social sound events as spatial boundaries, leaving agents prone to disturbing stationary conversations or colliding with NLOS dynamic pedestrians while over-optimizing to reach the acoustic target.

\subsection{Cross-Modal Spatial Memory and Mapping}
Long-horizon navigation necessitates structured memory. Purely visual architectures map geometric features to semantic nodes, like 3D scene graphs and open-vocabulary fields \cite{3DDynamicScene2020, ConceptFusionOpensetMultimodal2023, ConceptGraphsOpenvocabulary3D2024, BareQueriesOpenvocabulary2025, DynamemOnlineDynamic2025, ClioRealtimeTaskdriven2024, M3DMapObjectawareMultimodal2025}. While providing actionable topology, purely visual maps cannot register entities before they enter LOS. To incorporate audio, AVNav memory models accumulate historical trajectory features to prevent forgetting the acoustic target \cite{EchoenhancedEmbodiedVisual2023, LearningSetWaypoints2021}, or associate high-decibel audio with localized geometric grids under strict static environment assumptions \cite{MultimodalSpatialLanguage2025, SpatialCLAPLearningSpatiallyaware2025}. Similarly, probability-based scene graphs update regional beliefs from coarse audio directionality but stringently require predefined human-activity heatmaps to resolve ambiguities \cite{HomeEmergencyUsingAudio2025}. By contrast, our mapping module, SAVMap, eliminates these restrictions. It anchors transient, unconstrained sound events into the topological layout as inferred entities, and projects these non-visual inferences into a continuous social cost field. This effectively transforms multidirectional acoustic cues into persistent, geometry-aware boundaries for proactive planning.
