\section{Method}
\label{sec:method}

To tackle the profound challenges of perceiving non-line-of-sight (NLOS) human activities and planning under severe acoustic ambiguity, we introduce a modular, sensorimotor framework: \emph{Socially-Aware Audio-Visual Navigation (SAVNav)}. As illustrated in Fig.~\ref{fig:method_framework}, the system continuously processes egocentric sensory streams through a three-layer architecture: multi-modal perception, acoustic-to-spatial social mapping (SAVMap), and uncertainty-aware social planning.

\subsection{Multi-Modal Perception}
\label{subsec:method-perception}

The robot is equipped with a calibrated RGB-D camera and a 4-channel microphone array that captures first-order Ambisonics spatial audio. To support continuous closed-loop navigation, we decouple the high-frequency visual perception from the lower-frequency, temporal-context-dependent audio perception.

\textbf{Visual Perception.} At each simulation step, we employ an object detector (YOLO) coupled with depth filtering to identify humans within the line-of-sight (LOS). Let $\mathcal{H}_t = \{h_1, h_2, \dots\}$ denote the set of visually detected humans at time $t$. For each detected human $h_i$, we estimate its 3D position $\mathbf{p}_{v,i} \in \mathbb{R}^3$ in the world frame using the segmented depth mask. To model visual occlusion and depth sensor noise, we assign a spatial uncertainty covariance $\Sigma_{v,i}$ to each detection.

\textbf{Audio Perception.} To handle complex acoustic environments involving multiple simultaneous sources (e.g., target sound mixed with social chat), we utilize a Sound Event Localization and Detection (SELD) model based on the Embed-ACCDOA format. Operating on a 2.5-second sliding window of spatial audio, the model outputs a set of active sound events $\mathcal{A}_t = \{a_1, a_2, \dots\}$. Each acoustic event $a_j$ is characterized by its semantic class $c_j \in \{\texttt{doorbell}, \texttt{help}, \texttt{music}, \texttt{water}, \texttt{chat}, \texttt{footstep}\}$ and a 3D Direction-of-Arrival (DoA) unit vector $\mathbf{u}_j$ in the world frame. 

Crucially, raw DoA estimates lack depth information and suffer from severe angular variance due to room reverberation and geometric occlusions. Consequently, acoustic cues alone are insufficient for safe local planning, motivating the need for a persistent, multi-modal spatial representation.

\subsection{Acoustic-to-Spatial Social Mapping (SAVMap)}
\label{subsec:method-mapping}

The core mapping challenge in SAVNav is translating transient, depth-agnostic acoustic rays into persistent, spatially grounded cost fields that the planner can navigate around. We propose SAVMap, a novel spatio-temporal representation that maintains a probabilistic belief over both the task target and dynamic social boundaries.

\textbf{Sparse Belief Anchoring.} When a sound event $a_j$ is detected, SAVMap attempts to ground the acoustic DoA ray to the physical environment. For target sounds (\texttt{doorbell}, \texttt{music}, etc.), we maintain a particle filter over the navigable floor space, updating the belief distribution using a likelihood function modeled on the DoA vector $\mathbf{u}_j$ and environmental occlusion. 

For social sounds (\texttt{chat}, \texttt{footstep}), SAVMap first attempts to associate the sound with a visually detected human $h_i \in \mathcal{H}_t$ by minimizing the angular distance between the acoustic DoA $\mathbf{u}_j$ and the visual centroid $\mathbf{p}_{v,i}$. If a match is found (e.g., a visible person is talking), the social zone is anchored precisely to the visual detection, significantly reducing spatial uncertainty.

\textbf{Topology-Aware Social Propagation.} The most critical scenario arises when a social sound (e.g., an approaching footstep) has no matching visual entity. This indicates an NLOS risk---a human moving behind a corner or a door. Since the raw DoA only provides a bearing, projecting a simple cost function along the ray would incorrectly penalize walls and untraversable obstacles.

Instead, SAVMap projects the acoustic ray onto the environment's topological graph (extracted from the static obstacle map). Let $\mathbf{p}_{nlos}$ be the estimated intersection of the DoA ray with the nearest frontier or doorway. We model the unobserved human as a dynamic Gaussian cost distribution. For stationary social events (\texttt{chat}), we instantiate a static virtual boundary around $\mathbf{p}_{nlos}$. For dynamic social events (\texttt{footstep}), we apply a \emph{hallucinated momentum} algorithm. Specifically, the social cost field $\mathcal{C}_{soc}(\mathbf{x})$ at spatial location $\mathbf{x}$ propagates along the shortest geodesic paths of the topological graph towards the robot, anticipating the unseen human's trajectory:
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{C}_{soc}(\mathbf{x}, t) = \sum_{j \in \text{NLOS}} w_j \exp \left( -\frac{d_{geo}(\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{p}_{nlos, j} + \mathbf{v}_j \Delta t)^2}{2 \sigma_{soc}^2} \right)
\end{equation}
where $d_{geo}(\cdot, \cdot)$ is the geodesic distance function, $\mathbf{v}_j$ is the estimated velocity vector constrained by the local topology, and $\sigma_{soc}$ defines the radius of the social boundary. This mechanism uniquely enables the robot to "see" social boundaries through walls and proactively slow down or yield before a blind-corner collision occurs.

\subsection{Uncertainty-Aware Social Planning}
\label{subsec:method-planning}

Given the probabilistic target belief and the dynamic social cost fields provided by SAVMap, the final component is an uncertainty-aware navigation policy designed to solve the dual challenges of acoustic ambiguity and social compliance. 

Rather than strictly following the geometric shortest path to the maximum a posteriori (MAP) target estimate, our planner actively manages uncertainty. The total navigation cost $J(\pi)$ for a candidate path $\pi$ is formulated as a combination of distance, social intrusion, and information gain:
\begin{equation}
    J(\pi) = \underbrace{\alpha \int_{\pi} 1 \, ds}_{\text{Path Efficiency}} + \underbrace{\beta \int_{\pi} \mathcal{C}_{soc}(\mathbf{x}(s)) \, ds}_{\text{Social Compliance}} - \underbrace{\gamma \cdot \mathcal{I}(\pi | \mathcal{A})}_{\text{Active Search}}
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{I}(\pi | \mathcal{A})$ represents the expected information gain about the target's location from future acoustic observations along the path. 

When the target's acoustic ambiguity is high (e.g., heavy reverberation, Tier 2), the active search term dominates, guiding the robot to explore acoustically transparent vantage points that resolve the source's location. Simultaneously, the social compliance term creates a strong repulsive gradient around both visible humans and hallucinated NLOS boundaries. This proactive formulation ensures that the robot yields to impending footsteps or respectfully circumvents ongoing conversations, satisfying the rigorous safety criteria defined in the SAVNav benchmark without sacrificing task completion.
