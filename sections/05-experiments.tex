\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

We evaluate SAVNav on the benchmark introduced in Section~\ref{sec:benchmark}. Our experiments are designed to validate three core claims: 1) \textit{System-level:} Addressing the SAVNav task requires deep joint reasoning over visual and acoustic modalities, rather than relying on strong single-modality or socially-unaware tracking; 2) \textit{Mechanism-level (Time):} Instantaneous multi-modal reactions are insufficient; persistent spatial memory is required to handle occlusions and ambiguity; and 3) \textit{Mechanism-level (Space \& Search):} Specifically, topology-aware spatial inference is necessary for mitigating NLOS risks, and active information-seeking is necessary for resolving acoustic ambiguity.

\subsection{Experimental Setup}
\label{subsec:exp-setup}

We evaluate all methods on the SAVNav benchmark dataset defined in Section~\ref{sec:benchmark}, spanning the Crowded Social, Hidden Boundary, and Mixed Home Activity scenarios. The navigating agent is instantiated as a Stretch 3 robot\footnote{\url{https://hello-robot.com/stretch-3-product}}, providing a forward-facing RGB-D camera and a 4-channel Ambisonic microphone array. Visual observations ($I^{(t)}, D^{(t)}$) are acquired at $20\,\mathrm{Hz}$, while acoustic waveforms ($W^{(t)}$) are captured over a $2.55\,\mathrm{s}$ sliding window. The robot's kinematic limits are bounded by $S_{\mathrm{max}}=0.5\,\mathrm{m/s}$ and $S_{\mathrm{min}}=0.05\,\mathrm{m/s}$, with nominal exploration speed $v_{\mathrm{avg}}=0.25\,\mathrm{m/s}$. We configure conservative anticipatory velocity hypotheses corresponding to typical human motion ($v_{\mathrm{walk}}=1.0\,\mathrm{m/s}$). All methods operate under identical episode constraints ($T_{\mathrm{max}}=500$, $d_{\mathrm{success}}=1.0\,\mathrm{m}$) and are assessed via the benchmark metrics.
% Due to space constraints, we omit the exhaustive list of empirical tuning constants (e.g., hysteresis thresholds, cost field variances, and decay rates). A complete specification of these hyperparameters is provided in our open-source repository at [ANONYMOUS_REPO_LINK].

We compare SAVNav against three baselines, each adapted to expose a specific capability gap:

\begin{enumerate}
	\item \textit{Falcon+Goal Oracle:} Adapted from Falcon~\cite{CognitionPrecognitionFutureaware2025}, this baseline uses ground-truth target locations but purely vision-based human reasoning. It isolates the limits of LOS-only social intelligence, testing whether a system can succeed without treating \texttt{conversation} or \texttt{footsteps} as NLOS cues.
	\item \textit{ENMuS\textsuperscript{3}:} ENMuS\textsuperscript{3}~\cite{AudiovisualNavigationNoisy2025} is an audio-visual navigation framework. Evaluated on episodes with aligned BeDAViN categories, it isolates the limits of socially-unaware AVNav: it uses audio to pursue the target but ignores human-generated sounds as social boundaries.
	\item \textit{Reactive SAVNav:} This variant shares our exact multi-modal perception but disables all inter-frame accumulation ($\lambda=0$ in~\eqref{eq:nlos_score}, $S_{\mathrm{anch}}^{(t-1)}=0$ in~\eqref{eq:anch_update}, and no $\mathcal{B}(\mathbf{x})$ used by~\eqref{eq:utility}). It matches acoustic events to entities greedily per frame and projects waypoints at a fixed look-ahead $r_{react}=2.0\,\mathrm{m}$ along the target DOA $\mathbf{d}_{\mathrm{world}}$. It tests whether instantaneous multi-modal reactions are sufficient without persistent memory.
\end{enumerate}

Compared with Falcon+Goal Oracle, SAVNav highlights the vulnerability of LOS-only navigation in the face of hidden risks. In Hidden Boundary, Falcon completely fails to anticipate humans behind walls, resulting in a severe NCR of 48.73\% and a high overall HCR of 56.48\%. SAVNav utilizes $\mathcal{N}_{\mathrm{cand}}^{(t)}$ and $S_{\mathrm{nlos}}^{(t)}(n_j)$ in~\eqref{eq:cand_set} and~\eqref{eq:nlos_score} to safely yield, reducing NCR to 11.36\% and HCR to 17.85\%, while simultaneously increasing SR. This demonstrates that pure visual forecasting cannot safely manage blind-corner encounters without audio-derived NLOS structure.

Compared with ENMuS\textsuperscript{3}, SAVNav exposes the danger of socially-unaware AVNav. While ENMuS\textsuperscript{3} relies on acoustic tracking to find the target, it ignores the social boundaries formed by conversational groups and footsteps. This leads to profound failures in social compliance, with PSC dropping to 66.38\% in Crowded Social and 64.53\% in Mixed Home Activity. By explicitly constructing $C_{\mathrm{scg}}(\mathbf{x})$ and $C_{\mathrm{dp}}(\mathbf{x})$ in~\eqref{eq:scg_cost} and~\eqref{eq:dp_cost}, and using $S(\mathbf{x})$ in~\eqref{eq:speed}, SAVNav maintains high social compliance (PSC near 90\%) across all settings, proving that treating sound merely as a goal rather than a social boundary causes severe intrusions.

Compared with Reactive SAVNav, we validate the necessity of persistent map-level states. Both methods share identical perception, yet the reactive variant suffers from jittering trajectories and delayed yielding. This is especially evident in Mixed Home Activity, where the lack of an accumulated target belief map $\mathcal{B}(\mathbf{x})$ and persistent anchoring score $S_{\mathrm{anch}}^{(t)}$ (see~\eqref{eq:utility} and~\eqref{eq:anch_update}) drops SR by 13.07 points and SPL by 12.34 points. The rise in HCR across scenarios also confirms that instantaneous multi-modal reactions fail to establish stable yielding behaviors.

\subsubsection{w/o Topology-Aware Acoustic Anticipation}
This variant ablates the spatial dimension by removing topology-guided probability diffusion. It does not construct candidate nodes $\mathcal{N}_{\mathrm{cand}}^{(t)}$ in~\eqref{eq:cand_set} or evaluate $S_{\mathrm{nlos}}^{(t)}(n_j)$ in~\eqref{eq:nlos_score}. Instead, for an unanchored social acoustic event, it calculates the intersection of the DOA ray $\mathbf{d}_{\mathrm{world}}$ with the nearest static obstacle and places a single localized isotropic cost there, without inferring DP velocity hypotheses $\mathbf{v}_{j}$ in~\eqref{eq:vel_hyp}.

\textit{Results:} The degradation is most evident in Hidden Boundary, where NCR rises from 11.36\% to 25.63\% and HCR rises from 17.85\% to 31.24\%. This confirms that avoiding the raw acoustic direction is insufficient; blind-corner safety requires understanding from which topological node the risk will emerge. The same pattern appears in Mixed Home Activity, proving that NLOS risk must be represented as a structured emergence field rather than a single reactive point penalty.

\subsubsection{w/o Active Sensory Exploration}
This variant ablates the search dimension by removing information-theoretic utility evaluation. It does not maintain the target spatial belief map $\mathcal{B}(\mathbf{x})$ and disables $U(\mathbf{p}_i)$ in~\eqref{eq:utility}. If the acoustic target is unanchored, the robot executes a greedy pursuit: it bypasses viewpoint selection and assigns the nearest traversable point along the current target DOA $\mathbf{d}_{\mathrm{world}}$ as its immediate waypoint.

\textit{Results:} This ablation primarily impacts target-reaching efficiency rather than social safety. In Hidden Boundary, SR drops from 51.47\% to 39.62\% and SPL drops from 41.23\% to 29.74\%, while HCR remains largely unaffected. In Mixed Home Activity, SR drops by 12.38 points. This pattern confirms that when targets are acoustically ambiguous due to occlusions, greedy directional tracking fails; resolving ambiguity requires active information-seeking behavior.
