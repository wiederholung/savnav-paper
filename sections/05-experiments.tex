\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

We evaluate SAVNav on the dataset introduced in Section~\ref{sec:dataset}. Our experiments are designed to validate three core claims: 1) safe navigation requires joint audio-visual perception; relying on vision alone leads to blind-spot collisions, while socially-unaware audio trackers violate spatial norms; 2) maintaining persistent spatial states (i.e., accumulating target and risk beliefs over time) is essential to stabilize yielding and handle occlusions, exposing the limits of instantaneous reactive methods; and 3) resolving severe occlusions and acoustic ambiguity specifically requires proactive reasoning, via layout-guided anticipation and active information-seeking.

\subsection{Experimental Setup}
\label{subsec:exp-setup}

We evaluate all methods on the SAVNav dataset defined in Section~\ref{sec:dataset}, spanning the crowded social, hidden boundary, and mixed home activity scenarios. The navigating agent is instantiated as a Stretch 3 robot\footnote{\url{https://hello-robot.com/stretch-3-product}}, providing a forward-facing RGB-D camera and a 4-channel Ambisonic microphone array. Visual observations ($I^{(t)}, D^{(t)}$) are acquired at $20\,\mathrm{Hz}$, while acoustic waveforms ($A^{(t)}$) are captured over a $2.55\,\mathrm{s}$ sliding window. The robot's kinematic limits are bounded by $F_{\max}=0.5\,\mathrm{m/s}$ and $F_{\min}=0.05\,\mathrm{m/s}$, with nominal exploration speed $v_{\mathrm{avg}}=0.25\,\mathrm{m/s}$. We configure conservative anticipatory velocity hypotheses corresponding to typical human motion ($v_{\mathrm{walk}}=1.0\,\mathrm{m/s}$). All methods operate under identical episode constraints ($T=500$, $d_{\mathrm{success}}=1.0\,\mathrm{m}$) and are assessed via the dataset metrics.
% Due to space constraints, we omit the exhaustive list of empirical tuning constants (e.g., hysteresis thresholds, cost field variances, and decay rates). A complete specification of these hyperparameters is provided in our open-source repository at [ANONYMOUS_REPO_LINK].

We compare SAVNav against three baselines, each adapted to expose a specific capability gap:

\begin{enumerate}
	\item \textit{Falcon+Goal Oracle:} Adapted from Falcon~\cite{CognitionPrecognitionFutureaware2025}, this baseline uses ground-truth target locations but purely vision-based human reasoning. It isolates the limits of LOS-only social intelligence, testing whether a system can succeed without treating \texttt{conversation} or \texttt{footsteps} as NLOS cues.
	\item \textit{ENMuS\textsuperscript{3}:} ENMuS\textsuperscript{3}~\cite{AudiovisualNavigationNoisy2025} is an audio-visual navigation framework. Evaluated on episodes with aligned BeDAViN categories, it isolates the limits of socially-unaware AVNav: it uses audio to pursue the target but ignores human-generated sounds as social boundaries.
	\item \textit{Reactive SAVNav:} This variant shares our exact multi-modal perception but disables all inter-frame accumulation ($\lambda_{\mathrm{nlos}}=0$ in~\eqref{eq:nlos_score}, $B_{\mathrm{anch}}^{(t-1)}=0$ in~\eqref{eq:anch_update}, and no $B^{(t)}(\mathbf{x})$ used by~\eqref{eq:utility}). It matches sound events to entities greedily per frame and projects waypoints at a fixed look-ahead $r_{react}=2.0\,\mathrm{m}$ along the target DOA $\mathbf{d}_{\mathrm{aud}}^{(t)}$. It tests whether instantaneous multi-modal reactions are sufficient without persistent memory.
\end{enumerate}

Compared with Falcon+Goal Oracle, SAVNav highlights the vulnerability of LOS-only navigation in the face of hidden risks. In hidden boundary, Falcon completely fails to anticipate humans behind walls, resulting in a severe NCR of 48.73\% and a high overall HCR of 56.48\%. SAVNav utilizes $\mathcal{N}_{\mathrm{topo}}$ to infer the most probable emergence node $n^*$ and its risk score $B_{\mathrm{nlos}}^{(t)}(n^*)$ in~\eqref{eq:nlos_score} to safely yield, reducing NCR to 11.36\% and HCR to 17.85\%, while simultaneously increasing SR. This demonstrates that pure visual forecasting cannot safely manage blind-corner encounters without audio-derived NLOS structure. In the crowded social scenario, although Falcon+Goal Oracle achieves higher SR and SPL, this is an expected artifact of its access to ground-truth target coordinates, which eliminates the need for active exploration.

Compared with ENMuS\textsuperscript{3}, SAVNav exposes the danger of socially-unaware AVNav. While ENMuS\textsuperscript{3} relies on acoustic tracking to find the target, it ignores the social boundaries formed by conversational groups and footsteps. This leads to profound failures in social compliance, with PSC dropping to 66.38\% in crowded social and 64.53\% in mixed home activity. By explicitly constructing the context-aware fields $C_{\mathrm{nlos}}(\mathbf{x})$ in~\eqref{eq:scg_cost} and~\eqref{eq:dp_cost}, and aggregating them in~\eqref{eq:final_cost} before updating $F(\mathbf{x})$ in~\eqref{eq:speed}, SAVNav maintains high social compliance (PSC near 90\%) across all settings, proving that treating sound merely as a goal rather than a social boundary causes severe intrusions.

Compared with Reactive SAVNav, we validate the necessity of persistent map-level states. Both methods share identical perception, yet the reactive variant suffers from jittering trajectories and delayed yielding. This is especially evident in mixed home activity, where the lack of an accumulated target belief map $B^{(t)}(\mathbf{x})$ and persistent anchoring score $B_{\mathrm{anch}}^{(t)}$ (see~\eqref{eq:utility} and~\eqref{eq:anch_update}) drops SR by 13.07 points and SPL by 12.34 points. The rise in HCR across scenarios also confirms that instantaneous multi-modal reactions fail to establish stable yielding behaviors.

\subsection{Ablation Study}
\label{subsec:exp-ablation}

To isolate the contribution of each capability, we evaluate two ablated variants of SAVNav, shown in Table~\ref{tab:main_results}. Each variant removes one planner-relevant capability while keeping the rest of the system as fixed as possible, so that the performance drop can be attributed to a missing function rather than to a wholesale redesign.


\subsubsection{w/o Topology-Aware Acoustic Anticipation}
This variant ablates the spatial dimension by removing topology-guided probability diffusion. It does not utilize precomputed topological nodes $\mathcal{N}_{\mathrm{topo}}$ to extract the candidate set or evaluate $B_{\mathrm{nlos}}^{(t)}(n_j)$ in~\eqref{eq:nlos_score}. Instead, for an unanchored social sound event, it calculates the intersection of the DOA ray $\mathbf{d}_{\mathrm{aud}}^{(t)}$ with the nearest static obstacle and places a single localized isotropic cost there, without inferring DP velocity hypotheses $\mathbf{v}_{*}^{(t)}$ in~\eqref{eq:vel_hyp}.

\textit{Results:} The degradation is most evident in hidden boundary, where NCR rises from 11.36\% to 25.63\% and HCR rises from 17.85\% to 31.24\%. This confirms that avoiding the raw acoustic direction is insufficient; blind-corner safety requires understanding from which topological node the risk will emerge. The same pattern appears in mixed home activity, proving that NLOS risk must be represented as a structured emergence field rather than a single reactive point penalty.

\subsubsection{w/o Active Sensory Exploration}
This variant ablates the search dimension by removing information-theoretic utility evaluation. It does not maintain the target spatial belief map $B^{(t)}(\mathbf{x})$ and disables $U^{(t)}(\mathbf{p}_i)$ in~\eqref{eq:utility}. If the acoustic target is unanchored, the robot executes a greedy pursuit: it bypasses viewpoint selection and assigns the nearest traversable point along the current target DOA $\mathbf{d}_{\mathrm{aud}}^{(t)}$ as its immediate waypoint.

\textit{Results:} This ablation primarily impacts target-reaching efficiency rather than social safety. In hidden boundary, SR drops from 51.47\% to 39.62\% and SPL drops from 41.23\% to 29.74\%, while HCR remains largely unaffected. In mixed home activity, SR drops by 12.38 points. This pattern confirms that when targets are acoustically ambiguous due to occlusions, greedy directional tracking fails; resolving ambiguity requires active information-seeking behavior.

Fig.~\ref{fig:qualitative_results} illustrates a representative episode in a mixed scenario, demonstrating how SAVNav resolves acoustic ambiguity while maintaining social compliance. At step 11, the initial target belief map exhibits high spatial ambiguity regarding the source of $a_{\mathrm{target}}$. Concurrently, the robot identifies two LOS human actors ($h_1$ and $h_2$) belonging to an SCG and maps their corresponding social cost fields to constrain the initial FMM path. As the robot progresses through the corridor (step 168), continuous audio-visual observations reduce spatial uncertainty. The belief map contracts toward the distant room, guiding the designated best viewpoint forward. By step 201, an NLOS DP ($h_3$) begins moving forward and emitting footstep sounds from a blind corner. The robot perceives this acoustic cue and instantly generates a topology-aware social cost field at the doorway, leading the FMM planner to formulate a detour path. At step 226, the robot successfully circumvents $h_3$ and continues navigating toward the current best viewpoint, with the cost field dynamically updating in response to the DP's continuous movement. Finally, at step 337, after entering the room, the robot visually confirms the acoustic target ($h_4$) beside the bed and constructs audio-visual anchoring. The system directly transitions to precision navigation toward the target, yielding a terminal trajectory that closely aligns with the ground-truth shortest path.
