\title{SAVNav: Socially-Aware Audio Visual Navigation with Hallucinated Momentum and Active Search}

\input{sections/authors}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
Social navigation in human-populated environments requires robots to not only avoid collisions but also respect social norms and anticipate potential risks. Current approaches predominantly rely on vision, which is inherently limited to Line-of-Sight (LOS) perception, leaving robots vulnerable to ``corner surprise'' scenarios where dynamic agents appear suddenly from blind spots. In this work, we introduce SAVNav, a Socially-Aware Audio-Visual Navigation framework that leverages the omnidirectional nature of sound to address these Non-Line-of-Sight (NLOS) challenges. We propose a novel mechanism of ``Acoustic Hallucinated Momentum,'' which projects virtual risk fields into occluded regions based on audio direction-of-arrival, enabling the planner to execute anticipatory wide turns. Furthermore, to resolve the inherent ambiguity of acoustic signals, we design an information-theoretic Active Search strategy that guides the robot to effectively localize unseen sound sources. We integrate these methods into a unified high-fidelity simulation platform combining physical acoustics (SoundSpaces 2.0) with dynamic social agents (Habitat-Lab 3.0). Extensive experiments demonstrate that SAVNav significantly reduces NLOS collision rates and improves social compliance compared to state-of-the-art vision-only and audio-visual baselines. Finally, we validate our zero-shot framework on a Stretch 3 real robot, showcasing robust performance in complex acoustic environments without any additional training.
\end{abstract}
