\title{SAVNav: Socially-Aware Audio-Visual Navigation with Acoustic Hallucinated Momentum and Active Search}

\input{sections/authors}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
    Social navigation in crowded environments demands that robots not only avoid immediate collisions but also anticipate hidden risks. However, existing vision-centric approaches are fundamentally constrained by Line-of-Sight (LOS) perception, leaving robots vulnerable to ``corner surprises''---sudden encounters with dynamic agents emerging from blind spots. To overcome this limitation, we introduce \textbf{SAVNav}, a zero-shot Socially-Aware Audio-Visual Navigation framework that exploits the omnidirectional nature of sound to perceive Non-Line-of-Sight (NLOS) risks. Central to our approach is a novel mechanism termed ``Acoustic Hallucinated Momentum,'' which projects virtual risk fields into occluded regions based on audio Direction-of-Arrival (DoA), compelling the planner to execute anticipatory defensive maneuvers. To further address the spatial ambiguity of acoustic signals, we design an information-theoretic Active Search strategy that guides the robot to efficiently localize unseen sound sources. We evaluate SAVNav on a high-fidelity simulation platform integrating SoundSpaces 2.0 and Habitat-Lab 3.0. Extensive experiments demonstrate that SAVNav reduces NLOS collision rates by over 85\% and significantly improves social compliance compared to state-of-the-art vision-only and audio-visual baselines. Real-world deployment on a Stretch 3 robot confirms the framework's robust zero-shot transfer capabilities in complex, noisy acoustic environments.
\end{abstract}

\begin{IEEEkeywords}
    Social navigation, audio-visual perception, non-line-of-sight perception, active search, sim-to-real transfer
\end{IEEEkeywords}
