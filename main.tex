\title{Listen to Yield: Socially-Aware Audio-Visual Navigation via Acoustic Boundaries}

\input{authors}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
    Navigating crowded and dynamic environments requires robots to anticipate human behaviors and respect social boundaries. While vision-based social navigation and audio-visual navigation (AVNav) have seen significant progress, their integration remains an open challenge. Current vision systems fail to detect non-line-of-sight (NLOS) hazards, and AVNav approaches typically treat sound merely as a geometric goal rather than a social constraint. In this letter, we introduce socially-aware audio-visual navigation (SAVNav), a novel framework that empowers robots to locate acoustic targets while avoiding social sound events, such as conversations and footsteps, as spatial boundaries. We propose a three-stage pipeline comprising multi-modal perception, acoustic-to-spatial social mapping (SAVMap), and a proactive socially-aware navigation policy. Crucially, SAVMap resolves acoustic ambiguity by associating sounds with visual entities and leverages topological inference to anticipate NLOS humans. To evaluate this capability, we construct a dataset in Habitat 3.0 with realistic acoustic rendering. Experiments in simulation and on a physical robot demonstrate that SAVNav reaches acoustic targets both more successfully and more safely than vision-only and socially-unaware AVNav baselines, particularly by anticipating and mitigating NLOS collisions.
\end{abstract}
